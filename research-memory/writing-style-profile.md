# Writing Style Profile

Purpose: cross-skill memory for Geo Writing, Nature Polishing, and Personal Paper Style. Update only from user-approved comparisons between drafts and user revisions.

## Stable Writing Preferences

- Prefer concise, mechanism-forward geoscience prose.
- Keep claims proportional to evidence and design strength.
- Avoid inflated novelty language unless the contribution is explicitly demonstrated.
- Separate pattern, mechanism, limitation, and implication.
- Prefer clear quantitative sentences over decorative transitions.

## Section Tendencies

### Introduction

- Build from broad environmental relevance to a specific unresolved mechanism or inference gap.
- Avoid long background-only openings.

### Results

- Lead with the finding, then give comparison, magnitude, uncertainty, and context.
- Do not over-interpret observational associations in Results.

### Discussion

- Start from what the result changes conceptually or methodologically.
- State boundary conditions and likely mechanisms without overclaiming.

### Methods

- Explain why the design supports the inference, not only what software was used.

## Phrase Preferences

Prefer:

- "is consistent with"
- "suggests"
- "indicates"
- "we estimate"
- "we distinguish"
- "static pattern"
- "dynamic change"
- "counterfactual"
- "spatial leakage"

Avoid unless warranted:

- "prove"
- "unprecedented"
- "robust" without specifying the robustness test
- "leakage" when only pressure concentration or spatial gradient is shown
